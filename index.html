Welcome to Justus' Pi

 

This website, the one you're on right now, is being served off the Raspberry Pi pictured above, which is located in my bedroom in rural Minnesota.

"But what's a Raspberry Pi?" (click me)

It's just a very tiny, very cheap computer. Like "entree at a decent resaurant" cheap.

That's right, when you loaded this website, the packets that you sent across the internet from wherever you are ended up in my bedroom. Creepy.

I don't have a wired internet connection, just a wireless extender that connects to my landlord's wifi, which is the thing that the Pi is plugged into in the photo.

I'm using a neat service called PageKite. Basically, software running on the Pi reaches out and establishes a tunnel with PageKite's servers. PageKite then waits for incoming requests forwards them back across the tunnel to my Pi. Because the initial connection is outbound from my Pi, rather than inbound as with a normal server, I don't have to set up dynamic DNS, port forwarding, or any of that annoying difficult stuff.

This website will go down if my blind dog grabs the ethernet cable in her mouth and yanks the extender out of the wall. It has happened. It's right next to her bed.
